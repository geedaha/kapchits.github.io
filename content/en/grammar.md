Grammar & Syntax
Dr. Kapchits’s research on Somali syntax includes:

Sentence Structure: Analysis of SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) order, topic–focus particles, and case marking.
Morphological Features: Exploration of singular/plural forms, definiteness markers, and gender distinctions.
Clausal Relations: Use of relative clauses, subordination, and coordination in Somali narratives.

Syntax Examples:

“Ninka ayaa libaax dilay.”
Literal: “Man has lion killed.”
Free translation: “The man killed the lion.”
“Gabadhii ay ardayda ugu fiicneyd.”
Literal: “Girl that students most best.”
Free translation: “The girl who was the best among the students.”
